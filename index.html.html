Outdated security features of hardware and software
RVIII-006
Governance Major consequence Possible likelihood Avoid Updated
Event: Outdated security features leave hardware peripherals and software vulnerable to exploits.
Cause: Lack of awareness among users and administrators.
Consequence: Exposure to cyberattacks including malware, data breaches, and unauthorized access.
Hardware/software update notifications
Major (Likely)
2023–2025
Internet connection
Treatment: Prepare and implement a preventive maintenance plan for all hardware and software assets. Conduct training and planning sessions with ICT personnel.
Unauthorized access to information systems, websites, and databases
RVIII-007
Governance Moderate consequence Possible likelihood Avoid Ongoing
Event: Unauthorized access to IS, websites, database administration, and management systems.
Cause: Unsecured files and credentials.
Consequence: Moderate data exposure risk; no existing controls currently in place.
None identified
Major (Likely)
All year round
Internet connection
Treatment: Implement strong password policies, enable two-factor authentication (2FA), and deploy an identity access management (IAM) solution across all ICT systems.
Cybersecurity attack on ICT infrastructure
RVIII-008
Operational Major consequence Possible likelihood Avoid Controlled
Event: Cybersecurity attack on ICT division systems and data.
Cause: Vulnerabilities to malware, phishing, social engineering, and weak authentication.
Consequence: Data breaches exposing sensitive information; operational disruption and loss of productivity.
None identified
Major (Likely)
All year round
ICT personnel · Internet
Treatment: Conduct semi-annual cybersecurity awareness training; hire a cybersecurity specialist to coordinate with DICT monthly on vulnerabilities and exploits; implement a Security Operations Center (SOC).
